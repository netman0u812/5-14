3 — 167.69.251.252 (aetldap.aetna.com) not in IPAM

Single missing public Aetna IP — the root domain LDAP VIP. The two
aetldap.aetna.com entries (167.69.68.181 and 167.69.251.252) are F5 LDAP
load balancer VIPs. The .68.181 is in IPAM as Internal-HCB; .251.252 is
completely absent. Since 167.69.251.x is in the Aetna 167.69 block, this
can be added straightforwardly — but it's Internet-Facing or
Internal-HCB depending on whether this VIP is externally reachable.
Given it's the root aetna.com LDAP VIP, likely Internal-HCB. 

4 — Two DCs (10.80.232.21/22) resolving as Internal-Retail — wrong

HVCTHEHT01/02.healthehostt.com are HealtheHost TEST domain domain
controllers at 10.80.232.21/22. The IPAM LPM is matching them against a
retail store subnet (Heath TX - Store HEA). These are DCs, not retail
devices. This is the CONTESTED_BLOCK overlap problem in action —
10.80.232.0/24 is being claimed by retail_networks for store
allocations. These servers need their own IPAM entry with Internal-HCB
or Internal-PBM, and the /24 needs CONTESTED_BLOCK=Y.
